Class imbalance remains a persistent challenge in Intrusion Detection Systems (IDS), often causing machine learning models to favor majority attack types while overlooking rare but critical threats. To address this issue, we propose Manifold-guided Adaptive Centrality Oversampling (MACO), a novel oversampling framework that integrates manifold learning and topological marginality analysis to guide the generation of synthetic minority-class samples. Unlike conventional methods that rely solely on raw feature space or single-view embeddings, MACO constructs multiple manifold projections to capture heterogeneous structural patterns. The embedding quality of each projection is quantified using neighborhood preservation metrics. These quality scores then guide the assignment of adaptive, class-wise manifold weights, as well as the selection of structurally marginal yet informative samples for targeted oversampling. To further enhance minority class representation, MACO employs a neighborhood-guided interpolation strategy that generates synthetic instances near decision boundaries, thereby improving representational diversity. We conduct comprehensive evaluations on three widely used intrusion detection benchmarks—NSL-KDD, UNSW-NB15, and CICIDS2017—which span both classical and modern attack scenarios. Experimental results demonstrate that MACO consistently outperforms both traditional and state-of-the-art oversampling methods. It achieves significant improvements in macro-level F1-score, recall, and G-mean. Per-class performance analysis further reveals MACO’s ability to recover underrepresented attack types, highlighting its practical effectiveness in real-world imbalanced intrusion-detection scenarios. While processes like multi-manifold mapping introduce additional computational overhead, per-class analysis reveals MACO’s robust ability to recover underrepresented attacks. This highlights its practical effectiveness for imbalanced intrusion detection, though future optimizations will be necessary for strictly latency-sensitive and large-scale deployments.
